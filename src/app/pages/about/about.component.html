About This Website

Purpose of This Website?

Welcome to my website. I'm glad you are taking the time to check it out.

I hope to use this website as a platform to showcase my potential skills as I continue to learn. Hopefully, this will give clarity about who I am, my future endeavors, and my goals.

I also want to continue to expand my web development and front-end framework knowledge, and I believe maintaining a website will help with this greatly. I also plan to continue adding new features and content to this website which will all be software-related.

Overall, the end goal is to share new technologies and skills I have learned with others and improve my portfolio as time goes on.

About Me

Who Is This Guy?

I have been tinkering with and building computers since I was in high school. I work in software because I enjoy it, it is a hobby that simply turned into a career.

When it comes to software development I feel my creative mindset and work ethic can provide value to myself and others. I am eager to continue honing my skills and seeking out new opportunities to improve myself and provide more value.

For hobbies, I enjoy light gaming, jogging, and trying different craft beers here and there when I get the chance.

I love learning new things and tackling complicated challenges and hope to continue working in software throughout my career.

My Experience

What Have I Done?

I started out working at the University of North Texas (UNT) as an academic assistant. I met some amazing people and had many fantastic mentors.

This eventually led to me getting a job at Raytheon, (now Raytheon Technologies) as a college hire.

I work on personal projects in my free time. An example would be this website and other content to expand my skills.

I am focused mostly on Java development, but I try to learn more like the 'T' instead of an 'I'. What I mean by that is, an 'I' learner specializes in one or two things only. While a 'T' learner branches skills outward like an umbrella without worrying about tiny details that would not differentiate themselves.

Learning Angular for this website would be a good example. I'm not a web developer, but having JavaScript framework and web development knowledge is extremely important in today's world.
